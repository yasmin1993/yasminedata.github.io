By Yasmine Hassan


Palestinians mourn over bodies of a family killed in an Israeli airstrike on November 13,2019 ( AFP/Getty )

 The 14th of November 2019 was another sad day in Palestine; the lifeless bodies of five children, including two toddlers, were dug up from the rubble of their neighboring homes, tragically killed in yet another military airstrike targetting the war-scarred city of Gaza.

 Five new names found their place on the ever-growing list of innocent minors caught in the crossfire of the Palestinian-Israeli conflict, adding up to 29 lost lives this year alone, according to The Israeli Information Center for Human Rights in the Occupied Territories (B’Tselem). 

 Sadly, this year is actually not any special, but simply another node in the continuum of an unfortunate trend. Minors from both camps are constantly killed and wounded in the events of the ongoing war, with Palestinians making up a disproportionate number of the victims. 

 B’Tselem has been tracking all tragic incidents where Palestinian and Israeli minors, between 0 and 17 years old, were killed by Israeli soldiers and by Palestinians, respectively, over the past decade. The numbers tell a horrific, yet often forgotten, chapter in the story of an endless conflict.

An Unfortunate Trend

A total of 810 Palestinian and Israeli minors were killed in the Gaza Strip, the West Bank, and Israel since the beginning of February 2009 and until the end of November 2019, according to B’Tselem’s documentation.




 Coinciding with the Gaza War which witnessed a record spike in hostilities and destruction by both the Israeli state and Palestinian armed groups, ‘2014’ was an especially tragic year for Palestinian minors, accounting for over 70 percent of all fatalities over the past decade. 

 The Chair of The United Nations Independent Commission of Inquiry on the 2014 Gaza conflict had revealed to the press that “the extent of the devastation and human suffering in Gaza [during the 51-day operation in 2014] was unprecedented,” noting that one-third of Palestinian civilians killed in the events were children. 

 Nevertheless, although relatively smaller, the number of murdered children on any ‘regular’ year of the Palestinian-Israeli conflict is not any less alarming, especially on the Palestinian side. Putting aside the tragic year of 2014, at least one Palestinian minor was killed each month over the past decade, and an average of three per month.

A Disproportionate Conflict


It might not come as a surprise that the death toll in the conflict has been lopsided but the huge gap is nonetheless overwhelming, with Palestinian minors making up over 98 percent of the tragic deaths.  


The disparity becomes even more shocking if we take into consideration that the Israeli population since 2009 has been almost twofold that of the population in the Palestinian state, including the West Bank, the Gaza Strip and East Jerusalem, according to the most recent United Nations population estimates. Today, the Israeli population amounts to over eight and a half million, while the Palestinian population accounts for less than five million.





A Lose-Lose Scenario

Yet eventually, every lost soul from any of the two camps is one too many, whether it’s two-year-old Saher Salman killed by Israeli forces’ fire when playing outside his home; or few months old Chaya Zissel Braun who lost her life in a car attack by a Palestinian driver; or any other name on that never-ending list of tragedies.
Between bombed houses, car attacks, missile attacks, mortar shells and stabbings, Palestinians and Israeli children have been paying a very high price for the endless conflict.
According to B’Tselem’s documentation, most Palestinian minors killed over the past decade were victims of gunfire by Israeli soldiers, including missiles fired from aircraft, mortar fire, or rocket fires that either targetted the victim individually or ended up destroying their home or shelter leading to their death. Not any less tragic, Israeli children were victims of stabbing, gunfire, explosions, stone-throwing and car attacks.

A Cry for Help 


 Picturing that tragic scene where rescue workers dug up 15 months old Firas from the bloody debris of his home a few weeks ago, or the hundreds of times a Palestinian or Israeli mother has received the devastating news of a brutally murdered child in a military explosion or car attack, it is hard to imagine how cries for help from this never-ending conflict have been falling on deaf ears for decades.
“Israeli and Palestinian children grow up feeling that they are destined for conflict with their neighbors from the day they are born and there is little they or anyone else can do about it,” Award-winner author Laurel Holliday wrote in her 1999 book Children of Israel, Children of Palestine. Two decades later, the situation does not seem to be getting any better…
In a 2018 joint statement, the UN Humanitarian Coordinator in the Occupied Palestinian Territory (OPT), the Head of the UN Human Rights office (OHCHR) in the OPT and the Special Representative for the UN Children’s Fund (UNICEF) in the State of Palestine shared a “deep concern” about continuous incidents of murders and injuries of Palestinian children as well as the “fear, trauma, and grave injuries” facing the children of Israel.
But other than denouncements and warnings, the tragedy just goes on and the numbers keep adding up!
